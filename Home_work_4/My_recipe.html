How To Make Chicken Dum Biryani(By Nibedita swain)

What is Chicken Dum Biryani!

Chicken dum biryani is an evergreen classic that needs no introduction in parts of countries like Persia, India and others! Chicken dum biryani is goodness of rice and meat that comes in layers! The process of cooking in it’s own steam until rice is fluffy and meat cooked just to perfection is called ‘dum cooking’. A lengthy cooking process but all totally worth it!

Ingredients You Will Need:

For Rice:
  • 1 kg Basmati Rice, washed and rinsed
  • 4 Cloves
  • ½ inch Cinnamon
  • 2 Green Cardamom pods
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup Ghee Or melted Butter
For Marinade:
  • 1 kg chicken with bone, cleaned and washed
  • 4 medium onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp barista/fried onion
  • 1 tbsp saffron water
  • 2 sprigs of mint leaves
  • ½ cup curd, beaten
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp red chilli power
  • ½ tsp green chilli paste
  • 1 tbsp Ginger Garlic paste
  • 3-4 green chillies, slit
  • Salt to taste
Other Ingredients:
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • ¼ cup water
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • Few mint leaves
  • 1 tbsp barista
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tsp saffron water
  • ½ tsp rose water
  • Drop of kewra water

Process:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add chicken and marinate with all the ingredients. Let the chicken marinade preferably overnight or for minimum 3 hours.
  2. Let the rinsed rice rest for 20 minutes.
  3. Heat water in pot, add ghee and salt. Add cloves, cinnamon and green cardamom. Add rice and let it come to a boil. Immediately lower the flame and cook on low flame for 80%.
  4. In a heavy bottom pan, add ghee and marinated chicken. Cook for about 7-8 minutes.
  5. In another pan, layer the biryani. Add rice, chicken and then top it off with rice. Add in the chicken gravy on top.
  6. In the chicken’s pan, add water, milk, saffron water, ghee, mint leaves, barista, salt and coriander leaves. Add this mixer in the biryani. Add some more saffron water, rose water and few drops of kewra water. Now keep it on dum for 15-20 minutes on low flame.
